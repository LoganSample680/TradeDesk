Why I built TradeDesk

It started with my brother.

My brother is a painting contractor — one of the best I know. Fast, clean, the kind of work that gets him called back. But like a lot of great tradesmen, he'd give you the shirt off his back and let the paperwork pile up in the truck. The books had gotten away from him — far enough that neither of us really wanted to open them.

So one night we sat down and started digging out. The first version of TradeDesk did exactly one thing: scan it all in — income, expenses, receipts — and get the numbers straight. But the more we cleaned up, the clearer it got that the mess wasn't the real problem. The real problem was that none of the tools built for contractors were built to keep a good man out of that mess in the first place.

So we kept going. One feature at a time — estimates, e-sign, invoicing, payments, mileage, materials, 1099s — until TradeDesk stopped being a cleanup tool and became the thing that makes sure it never happens again. The books it keeps are clean enough to stand up to a full IRS audit. That's the whole point: you do the work you're great at, and the business takes care of itself.
